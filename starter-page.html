Starter Page



In the quiet corner of the university library, we often found ourselves immersed in discussions about technology and a shared fascination for how websites worked. What began as a casual interest soon turned into a serious passion when we, while browsing the internet, stumbled upon a poorly designed website that he jokingly called "a crime against web design." I laughed but remarked, "Why don’t we learn how to do it better?" That simple question marked the beginning of the journey. We started attending coding meetups and watching free tutorials on platforms like YouTube. Neither of us had any formal training in programming, but the determination to learn was undeniable. Armed with laptops and curiosity, we spent late nights experimenting with HTML and CSS, the building blocks of web design, and trying to recreate favorite websites




As skills grew, so did opportunities. we started receiving inquiries from other small businesses, impressed by the elevator agency's website. To handle the influx of projects, we officially branded ourselves as a team, naming the startup "Nexus." Balancing studies and freelancing wasn’t easy, but we managed by creating a schedule that allowed us to meet deadlines without compromising academic performance. We also began attending hackathons and networking events, meeting other developers and industry experts. These connections proved invaluable, as we learned new tools and techniques, like version control systems and frameworks like React, which made work more efficient. With every project, knowledge expanded, and the client base grew



One pivotal moment in the journey was landing an internship with a prominent web development agency during the final year of college. The experience was transformative. We honed UI/UX skills, learning how to create designs that not only looked good but also enhanced user experiences. Meanwhile, we also delved deeper into backend technologies, mastering frameworks like Node.js and deploying websites on cloud platforms. Working alongside seasoned developers, we discovered the importance of collaboration and adhering to industry standards. Mentors were impressed by our enthusiasm and dedication, often entrusting responsibilities beyond our roles. By the end of the internship, we had not only gained invaluable experience but also established a reputation as promising web developers
